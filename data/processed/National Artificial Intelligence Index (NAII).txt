1. Document Purpose
This document aims to introduce the National Al Index (NAII) by outlining its objectives and the
methodology adopted in its development. It also covers the key components on which the Index relies to
assess the readiness of government entities to adopt Al technologies. The document presents the
maturity levels for Al adoption by government entities, providing a detailed description of each level,
then explains the evaluation questions included in the Index, and concludes with the mechanism for
analyzing results.
2. Index Overview
The National Al Index (NAII) aims to assess the readiness level of government entities to adopt Al
technologies, monitor their progress on a periodic basis, and provide practical recommendations on
strengths and potential areas for improvement for each entity, thereby supporting the development and
use of these technologies at the national level. The Index consists of three main pillars, seven sub-pillars,
and twenty-three domains to ensure a comprehensive measurement process.
The design of the Index considers current challenges and future opportunities faced by government
entities in adopting Al. The most notable of these challenges include weak alignment between Al
initiatives and national priorities, limited availability of suitable data for training, variations in the
readiness of technical infrastructure across entities, a shortage of specialized national talent, and a lack
of unified and clear measurement standards.

Index Objectives:
- Aligning government efforts and priorities in the field of Al.
- Providing and improving the necessary enablers for Al adoption within government entities.
- Enabling the launch and adoption of Al products in key sectors and ensuring impact realization.
The Index delivers results that reflect each entitys adoption level while also providing the necessary
support and recommendations to strengthen their capabilities in developing effective and sustainable
Al solutions and applications that directly contribute to achieving the objectives of Saudi Vision 2030.
Additionally, the Index aims to empower government entities to develop Al products in priority sectors,
provide the essential enablers to support the sustainability of these efforts, and maximize their impact.
3. Index Development Methodology
The development of national indices is one key tool for strengthening the governance of emerging
technologies and guiding efforts toward achieving strategic objectives. With the rapid expansion in Al
adoption across both the public and private sectors, there is a clear need to design a national index that
reflects entities readiness to adopt Al and identifies opportunities for advancement in this vital field.
The methodology for developing the Index is based on three interconnected stages aimed at achieving
comprehensiveness and accuracy in building the Index, including surveying and analyzing benchmark
studies, designing the Index by defining its key components and measurement mechanism, and
performing measurement procedures. This process includes conducting workshops, completing and

3.2 Design
The design phase of the National Al Index aims to build a comprehensive and flexible measurement tool
that reflects the reality of entities in terms of strategic direction, internal readiness, and achieved
outcomes and impact. The design process considered alignment with the local context and leveraged
global best practices to ensure the reliability of results and their applicability across various sectors.
3.2.1 Index Framework
The Index consists of three main pillars: Directions, Enablers, and Outputs. These pillars include seven
main sub-pillars, divided into 23 sub-domains covering strategic, regulatory, technical, operational, and
human aspects. These components were carefully selected to provide a comprehensive and integrated
view of entities readiness levels in the field of Al.

A. Directions:
Strategy
Leading entities adopt ambitious strategies to deeply integrate Al into their operations, with the aim of
enhancing operational efficiency, reducing costs, and delivering personalized services. These entities
focus on leveraging Al to achieve a competitive advantage, foster innovation, and improve service quality
through advanced analytics.
Such strategies outline how the organization harnesses Al to achieve its objectives, defining priorities,
requirements, actions, timelines, and the governance framework necessary for sustainable
implementation. This sub-pillar covers:
Planning and Performance
Formulating the strategy and vision serves as the enterprise reference for directing Al efforts
toward measurable objectives and identifying priority domains. This contributes to building a
shared understanding, linking initiatives to enterprise objectives, and ensuring alignment with
national strategies and Saudi Vision 2030.
Initiatives
Initiatives serve as a tool of translating the strategic vision into practical projects that deliver
tangible impact. It includes operational and experimental initiatives aimed at improving
performance, such as process automation and the delivery of intelligent recommendations.

Budget
This refers to the dedicated budget for implementing Al initiatives within the entity, which is
essential for turning plans into outcomes. It reflects a strategic commitment in supporting digital
transformation.
Governance
As Al adoption develops, governance becomes a necessity to ensure the responsible and safe use of
technologies. Governance frameworks define roles, responsibilities, and decision-making mechanisms,
striking a balance between innovation, transparency, and compliance, thereby enhancing public trust
and achieving enterprise excellence. Al governance encompasses a set of frameworks, policies, and
enterprise structures that regulate the development and use of Al to ensure compliance and promote
safe, responsible use through periodic reviews and the adoption of leading local and global practices.
This includes:
Frameworks and Policies
Regulatory frameworks form the foundation for effective governance. They support the
organized use of smart technologies, strengthen public trust, and consider cultural and
developmental specificities.
Regulatory Enablement
This includes establishing a regulatory unit dedicated to Al within the entity to strengthen
governance and enterprise transformation, along with clearly defining its mandates.

Reliability and Safety
This involves ensuring the accuracy and stability of Al systems through control procedures,
adherence to national standards, and mitigation of potential risks, in alignment with relevant
regulatory frameworks.
Regulatory Compliance
Regulatory compliance requires embedding regulations into daily operations rather than treating
them as a mere formality. This includes implementing regulatory frameworks issued by SDAIA,
such as the Generative Artificial Intelligence Guidelines and Deepfake Guidelines.
B. Enablers
Data
Data forms the foundation of all Al applications, with the quality of outcomes highly dependent on the
accuracy, availability, and integration of data. Enablement involves building a robust enterprise
structure, enhancing data quality, and ensuring privacy and security, thereby supporting the
sustainability and efficiency of Al models. This includes:
Availability and Accessibility
Ensuring that data is available and easily accessible through the use of data discovery tools, data
catalogs, and automated updates to maintain currency.
Quality and Integration
Data quality directly affects model accuracy. Therefore, it is essential to track data sources,
document usage rights, conduct legal reviews for data usage, and ensure compliance with
intellectual property rights.

Infrastructure

Human Capabilities
Human capabilities are the cornerstone of Al adoption, requiring specialized technical expertise in Al
design, development, operation, and governance. Human enablement involves attracting top talent,
training skilled professionals, building career pathways, and providing a flexible, motivating work
environment, in addition to fostering academic partnerships to prepare the next generation of
professionals. This includes:
Headcount and Diversity
Effective enterprise adoption requires building diverse teams with deep expertise, aligned with
the National Occupational Standard Framework for Data and Artificial Intelligence.
Professional Development
Keeping pace with digital transformation demands a sustainable training plan that supports
continuous learning, aligned with job roles, and offering accredited certifications in the field.
Academic Collaboration
Partnering with universities and institutes helps supply the entity with qualified talent and
fosters an applied learning environment. It also facilitates attracting talented students through
training programs and technology competitions.
Job Stability
In the competitive market for specialized talent, retaining such expertise is a strategic priority.
This can be achieved through analyzing employee attrition causes and developing professional
and financial incentives, as well as aligning HR policies with the National Occupational Standard
Framework.

C. Outputs
Applications
Possessing in-house capabilities to develop Al-based products and applications is one of the most
significant indicators of enterprise maturity in this domain.
Development and Deployment
This includes the number of Al applications developed, as well as the development and
deployment of intelligent applications that provide tailored solutions to meet the entitys needs
through an internal development environment supporting model lifecycle management using
frameworks such as MLOps.
Privacy and Security
The entity adopts a Security and Privacy by Design approach, integrating encryption and access
controls from the earliest stages of analysis. It complies with national regulations, such as the
Personal Data Protection Law, and implements immediate recovery plans while conducting
periodic risk measurements to ensure the safety and continuity of applications.
Operation and Management
Operation involve tracking model usage and linking performance to enterprise indicators, such
as service quality and cost reduction. Intelligent monitoring mechanisms are implemented for
proactive improvement, and analytical reports are submitted to senior management to support
decision-making and enhance investment in successful models.

Impact
The impact of Al extends from cost reduction and efficiency enhancement to improved user experience
and more accurate, effective decision-making. This impact can only be realized when Al technologies
are integrated into the enterprise environment in alignment with strategy, governance, and enablers.
Operational Efficiency
Al contributes to improving operational efficiency and productivity by reducing costs through
automating manual processes. The impact is measured using return on investment (ROI) models
that include productivity gains, time savings, and performance improvements, directing
investments toward higher-value solutions. Al supports at least 25 of jobs, with productivity
gains targeted at over 15. Performance analysis tools are used to monitor this improvement
and link job training to actual outcomes.
Employee Productivity
Al is employed to analyze beneficiary behavior and personalize services, enhancing user
experience and improving time-related performance indicators by over 20. Interactivity is
further strengthened through proactive solutions such as chatbots, with Al integrated across all
service channels.
Service Quality and Improvement
This reflects the extent to which the entity adopts Al to deliver interactive, personalized, and
proactive services through diverse channels for users, customers, or citizens. Al powers more
than 25 of services, with improvements in response time, satisfaction, and personalization
exceeding 20.

3.3 Measurement
The percentage of Al adoption by entities is measured in three main steps, beginning with the delivery
of introductory workshops, followed by the distribution of questions for completion along with the
collection of supporting documents, and concluding with the analysis and verification of responses.
3.3.1 Awareness Workshops
Workshops are conducted with representatives of government entities to raise awareness of the
importance of Al adoption, introduce the National Al Index and its objectives, and explain the questions
in detail. The workshops also clarify the response mechanism and the process for compiling and sharing
supporting documents.
3.3.2 Questionnaire and Supporting Documents
The National Al Index questions are shared with government entities for completion. Entities are also
required to compile and share the documents and evidence supporting each response.
3.3.3 Review and Verification
Responses and supporting documents are reviewed and verified for accuracy and validity by a
specialized team. Follow-up workshops with entities may be held, where necessary, to address any
points requiring clarification.

3.4 Results
This stage represents the final step in implementing the National Al Index. It aims to accurately portray
the readiness level of government entities for Al adoption. It relies on data collected during the
measurement stage, transforming practices and indicators into quantitative results and benchmark
comparisons that enable the identification of gaps and guide future development plans.
3.4.1 Adoption Scores
The adoption score reflects the overall level attained by the entity in utilizing and implementing Al
technologies, based on an analysis of its enterprise performance results in this domain. The score is
calculated by aggregating and analyzing the results of the sub-domains within the Index components,
applying the predetermined weights for each domain. This approach enables the development of a
tailored roadmap to raise the level of adoption and determine the entitys position on the maturity scale.
3.4.2 Comprehensive and Detailed Reports
The reporting stage of the National Al Index plays a pivotal role in measuring Al adoption among entities.
These reports aim to support decision-makers in government entities by providing an accurate
analytical reading of the current state of adoption, enabling entities to set their development priorities
based on reliable data.

This stage includes two types of reports:
Comprehensive National Report:
Provides a holistic view of Al adoption levels across the government sector, highlighting areas
that have achieved advanced maturity, as well as identifying aspects requiring development or
intervention to strengthen enterprise readiness.
Detailed Report for Each Entity:
Based on the measurement results, SDAIA will analyze each entitys performance across
various domains and provide targeted recommendations that outline practical steps for
improving adoption levels. These recommendations will focus on addressing weaknesses and
maximizing the impact of Al utilization. The recommendations aim to:
Support decision-making within government entities through an objective analysis of
the adoption state.
Enhance the entities ability to develop future improvement plans with greater accuracy
and alignment to national priorities.
Raise competitiveness and encourage healthy competition among government entities
in adopting Al.
Achieve national alignment in progress toward the objectives of the National Strategy
for Data and Al.
