## 3. Findings (Retrieved Evidence)

### 3.1 NIST AI Risk Management Framework — Retrieved Evidence

The Core of the NIST AI RMF (Part 2) is composed of four high-level functions:

- **GOVERN** — Cultivates and maintains a culture of risk management; a cross-cutting function designed to inform and be infused throughout the other three functions. Governance sets systematic documentation practices, allocates risk resources, establishes expert consultation processes, and directs continual improvement and regular monitoring.
- **MAP** — Recognizes context and identifies risks related to that context.
- **MEASURE** — Assesses, analyzes, or tracks identified risks.
- **MANAGE** — Prioritizes and acts upon risks based on projected impact; applies governance-defined allocations to respond to, recover from, and communicate about incidents.

Each function is broken down into categories and subcategories, subdivided into specific actions and outcomes. These are not a checklist and are not necessarily ordered steps. Risk management using the Core should be continuous, timely, and performed throughout the AI system lifecycle.

Related indexed references also cite ISO documents in a definitional role only:
- **ISO 26000:2010** — definition of social responsibility (organization's responsibility for impacts of decisions/activities on society and environment through transparent and ethical behavior).
- **ISO/IEC TR 24368:2022** — definitions of sustainability and professional responsibility for AI designers/developers/deployers.

These are referenced as supporting definitions within NIST AI RMF context, not as governance standards themselves.
