## Scope and Objective
This report compares ISO 42001 (AI management system standard) with the NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF) for compliance purposes. The objective is to identify points of comparison, overlap, and divergence based on the indexed knowledge base.

## Findings

### Retrieved Evidence
1. **NIST AI RMF structure (retrieved evidence):** The Core of the NIST AI Risk Management Framework is composed of four high-level functions:
   - **GOVERN:** Cultivates and maintains a culture of risk management; a cross-cutting function designed to inform and be infused throughout the other three functions.
   - **MAP:** Recognizes the context and identifies risks related to that context.
   - **MEASURE:** Assesses, analyzes, or tracks the identified risks.
   - **MANAGE:** Prioritizes and acts upon risks based on projected impact.
   Each function is broken down into categories and subcategories, further subdivided into specific actions and outcomes. These actions are not a checklist and are not necessarily an ordered set of steps. Risk management should be continuous, timely, and performed throughout the AI system lifecycle.

2. **NIST AI RMF governance alignment (retrieved evidence):** The GOVERN function is a cross-cutting governance function intended to inform and be infused throughout MAP, MEASURE, and MANAGE across all stages of an organization's AI risk management processes. NIST has stated it will continue to align the AI RMF and related guidance, including governance aspects, with applicable international standards, guidelines, and practices as the Framework is updated. However, the indexed material does not specify particular ISO standards or explicit ISO control mappings for the NIST AI RMF governance function.

3. **ISO 42001 (retrieved evidence):** No indexed content was found describing ISO 42001, ISO/IEC 42001, its requirements, clauses, certification scheme, or AI management system structure. Multiple targeted searches returned no relevant material.

### Inference
- Because the indexed knowledge base contains no ISO 42001 content, a substantive side-by-side comparison of specific clauses, control requirements, certification obligations, or structural alignment between ISO 42001 and the NIST AI RMF cannot be produced from indexed evidence at this time.
- The NIST AI RMF is a voluntary framework organized around four functions rather than a certifiable management system standard; the indexed evidence does not establish whether ISO 42001 shares this structure.
