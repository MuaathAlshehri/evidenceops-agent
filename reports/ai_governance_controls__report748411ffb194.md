## Objective
Summarize evidence-grounded AI governance controls based on the indexed knowledge base, for approved saving as a final report.

## Findings

### Retrieved Evidence
The following AI governance controls are identified in the indexed knowledge base (NIST AI Risk Management Framework and associated guidance):

1. **Training and competency** — Personnel and partners receive AI risk management training to enable them to perform their duties and responsibilities consistent with related policies, procedures, and agreements.
2. **Executive accountability** — Executive leadership takes responsibility for decisions about risks associated with AI system development and deployment.
3. **Diverse and multidisciplinary input** — Decision-making related to mapping, measuring, and managing AI risks throughout the lifecycle is informed by a diverse team (e.g., diversity of demographics, disciplines, experience, expertise, and backgrounds), with workforce diversity, equity, inclusion, and accessibility processes prioritized.
4. **Roles, responsibilities, and oversight** — Policies and procedures are in place to define and differentiate roles and responsibilities for human-AI configurations and oversight of AI systems.
5. **Safety-first culture** — Organizational policies and practices are in place to foster a critical thinking and safety-first mindset in the design, development, deployment, and uses of AI systems to minimize potential negative impacts.

### Framework Context (Retrieved Evidence)
The NIST AI Risk Management Framework defines four functions: **GOVERN, MAP, MEASURE, and MANAGE**. GOVERN is a cross-cutting function that applies to all stages of an organization's AI risk management processes and informs the other three functions. Governance should be integrated into each of the other AI risk management functions and executed continuously across the AI system lifespan and organizational hierarchy.

- Governing authorities determine overarching policies directing mission, goals, values, culture, and risk tolerance.
- Senior leadership sets the tone for risk management and culture.
- Management aligns technical AI risk management to policies and operations.
- Documentation should enhance transparency, human review, and accountability.

### Inference
The five listed controls map to the GOVERN function of the NIST AI RMF: they establish accountability (executive leadership), competency (training), inclusive decision-making (diverse team), structural clarity (roles and oversight), and cultural posture (safety-first mindset). Together they represent foundational organizational governance prerequisites before system-specific MAP, MEASURE, and MANAGE activities.

### Recommendation
Organizations should treat these controls as continuous and intrinsic governance requirements rather than one-time checklists, and should document how each control is implemented to support transparency, human review, and accountability.
