## Findings

**NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF)**

**Objective:** To help organizations address risks associated with AI systems through a comprehensive risk management framework that evolves with technology, standards, and community feedback.

**Scope:** Broad organizational-level framework covering all AI systems, with focus on establishing organizational accountability, roles, and supportive culture for effective risk management. It addresses AI risks as part of broader enterprise risk management.

**Key Principles:**
1. **Trustworthy AI characteristics:** Valid and reliable, safe, secure and resilient, accountable and transparent, explainable and interpretable, privacy-enhanced, and fair with managed biases
2. **Core functions:** GOVERN (applies across all stages), MAP, MEASURE, and MANAGE (used in specific AI system contexts and lifecycle stages)
3. **Integration:** Designed to be used alongside other guidance and frameworks, leveraging existing standards like NIST Cybersecurity Framework, NIST Privacy Framework, and Secure Software Development Framework

**Strengths:**
- Comprehensive organizational approach with senior-level commitment emphasis
- Broad audience applicability requiring diverse perspectives across AI lifecycle
- Evolves with technology and international practices
- Focuses on both positive and negative impacts of AI systems

**Limitations:**
- Using the framework alone doesn't automatically create necessary accountability mechanisms or incentive structures
- Small to medium-sized organizations may face different implementation challenges
- Requires dedicated organizational change beyond framework adoption

**Typical Use Cases:** Organizational AI risk management programs, enterprise AI governance, compliance with AI regulations, integration with existing risk management processes

**OWASP LLM Top 10**

**Objective:** To provide security guidelines for developing and deploying Large Language Model applications by addressing common vulnerabilities and promoting secure practices.

**Scope:** Specific application security guidelines focused on Large Language Models, shaped by real-world feedback and contributions from global security professionals.

**Key Principles:**
1. **Zero-trust approach:** Treat the model as any other user
2. **Secure coding practices:** Input validation, sanitization, and output encoding
3. **Minimal permissions:** Limit extension functionality to only necessary actions with granular permissions
4. **Defense in depth:** Multiple security controls including Content Security Policies, parameterized queries, logging, and monitoring

**Strengths:**
- Specific to LLM application security vulnerabilities
- Based on real-world feedback and community contributions
- Practical, actionable security controls
- Addresses emerging LLM-specific threats like prompt injection

**Limitations:** The indexed evidence does not specify limitations regarding evidence collection
