# AI Governance Controls – Final Report

**Research Identifier:** report-ae2ff08b5a21
**Status:** Final (approved for saving)

## Retrieved Evidence
The following controls are drawn from indexed knowledge base sources, including the NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF) governance function and related guidance.

### 1. Data Governance and Minimization
- **Data Governance** covers all stages of the data life cycle, including security, usability, availability, and privacy. It requires defined data-handling policies and processes along with assigned authority and responsibilities for data management.
- **Data Minimization** limits collection of personal information strictly to what is needed to meet a specified objective.

### 2. Personnel, Leadership, and Accountability
- Personnel and partners must receive AI risk management training so they can carry out duties per relevant policies, procedures, and agreements.
- Executive leadership is responsible for decisions on risks tied to AI system development and deployment.
- Governing authorities set policies for mission, goals, values, and risk tolerance; senior leadership sets the tone for risk management and organizational culture; management aligns technical AI risk management with policies and operations.

### 3. Diversity, Equity, Inclusion, and Roles
- Decisions on mapping, measuring, and managing AI risks across the lifecycle should be informed by a diverse team (varied demographics, disciplines, experience, expertise, and backgrounds).
- Policies and procedures must define and differentiate roles and responsibilities for human-AI configurations and oversight of AI systems.

### 4. Organizational Culture
- The organization should maintain policies and practices that promote a critical thinking and safety-first mindset during the design, development, deployment, and use of AI systems to reduce potential negative impacts.
- Governance is a cross-cutting, continual function integrated across the AI system lifespan and organizational hierarchy, driving internal practices and norms that facilitate an organization's risk culture.

## Inference
The four control areas above represent a coherent governance baseline: data stewardship, accountable leadership and trained personnel, inclusive risk decision-making with clear roles, and a sustained safety-first culture. These align with the NIST AI RMF treatment of governance as a continual, cross-cutting function rather than a one-time activity.

## Recommendation
- Adopt the four control areas as foundational AI governance controls.
- Document roles, responsibilities, and data-handling policies to enhance transparency and accountability.
- Continue executing the govern function as organizational knowledge, cultures, and expectations evolve.
- Consider the NIST AI RMF Playbook for tailored, context-specific governing practices.
